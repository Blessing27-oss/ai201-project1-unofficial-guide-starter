Source: The Balance Money — First Apartment Checklist

---

BEFORE YOU SIGN THE LEASE

Budget check: Calculate total move-in costs including first month, last month, security deposit, application fee, and moving expenses. Confirm all items are within budget before applying.

Utility confirmation: Ask which utilities are included in rent and get the list in writing. For excluded utilities, ask the landlord or current tenant for average monthly costs.

Lease review: Read the full lease — all pages, all attachments, all addenda. Never sign a lease with blank fields. Anything agreed verbally should be written into the lease or into a signed addendum before you sign.

Background check: Search the landlord's name and the property address for complaints, lawsuits, housing court records, or news coverage. Many cities have online databases of housing code violations.

---

MOVE-IN INSPECTION CHECKLIST

Kitchen:
- Stove burners and oven work at all settings
- Refrigerator maintains temperature, door seals are intact
- Dishwasher runs a full cycle without leaking
- Garbage disposal works
- Under-sink cabinet shows no water damage or mold
- Outlets near sink are GFCI-protected

Bathroom:
- Hot water reaches adequate temperature within 60 seconds
- Shower pressure is acceptable
- Toilet flushes and refills completely without running
- Bathroom fan works
- No mold visible in grout, caulk, or ceiling corners
- Under-sink cabinet shows no staining or moisture

Bedroom and living areas:
- All outlets work (test with a phone charger)
- Windows open, close, and lock properly
- Closet doors open and close without sticking
- No stains on carpet or damage on hardwood
- No holes or large marks on walls beyond minor scuffs
- Smoke detector present, functioning, and not expired
- Carbon monoxide detector present if there's gas in the unit

General building:
- Entry doors lock securely
- Hallways and common areas are well-lit
- Laundry machines work (run a quick cycle)
- Parking space is clearly assigned in writing
- Mailbox is functional and lockable

---

AFTER MOVE-IN

Documentation: Send move-in photos and video to your landlord by email within 24 hours. Keep a copy. This creates a timestamped record of pre-existing conditions.

Utilities setup: Connect electricity, gas, internet within the first week. Some utilities require a few days to activate — schedule this before you actually need them.

Renter's insurance: Purchase a policy before you move belongings in. Coverage starts the date of purchase. Policies are available through most major insurers and often cost less than $20/month.

Maintenance reporting: Learn your landlord's preferred method for reporting maintenance issues (email, app, phone). Always use written communication so there's a record. Report issues promptly — delayed reporting can be interpreted as acceptance of the condition.

Emergency contacts: Locate the shutoffs for water, gas, and electricity in your unit and the building. Know your landlord's emergency contact number.

---

BEFORE YOU MOVE OUT

Notice requirements: Review your lease for required notice period before move-out (typically 30-60 days). Failing to give proper notice can result in additional fees or liability for extra rent.

Cleaning: Clean the unit to the standard it was in when you moved in. Normal wear and tear cannot be charged against your deposit; damage can.

Final walkthrough: Request a final walkthrough with your landlord before you leave the keys. This gives both parties a chance to identify any issues and agree on their status.

Forwarding address: Provide your landlord with a forwarding address in writing so they can return your deposit by mail. Your landlord will need this to comply with deposit return requirements.
