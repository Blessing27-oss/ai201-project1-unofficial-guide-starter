Source: Yelp — Student reviews of apartment complexes near university campuses

---

Hillcrest Apartments — 1 star
The management here is an absolute nightmare. I gave 45 days notice when I moved out (lease only required 30), left the unit spotless, took photos of everything, and they still tried to charge me $800 for "cleaning fees" and "touch-up painting." I had photos showing the unit was in perfect condition. Fought it, got my full deposit back minus $0. But the fact that I had to fight at all tells you everything.

---

The Heights — 4 stars
Honestly pretty good. Close to the bus line that goes to campus, management actually calls you back, and the property is well-maintained. The main thing I'd warn people about is the parking situation — there are 15 spots for 40 units, so if you have a car you need to get on the waitlist immediately. Without a car it's a great spot.

---

Westwood Commons — 2 stars
Loved the unit, hated the management. Every maintenance request took 2-3 weeks. The dishwasher was broken for six weeks. I had to follow up four times before anyone came to look at it. When they finally sent someone, it was fixed in 30 minutes. Why it took six weeks of chasing to get 30 minutes of labor, I will never understand. If you rent here, document every request in writing and follow up in writing every week.

---

The Brickhouse — 5 stars
Three years here and I have zero complaints. The landlord is a local family, not a management company, and they actually care about the building. When the boiler went out last February (right before midterms), they had it fixed by morning and sent an apology email. Rent has gone up slightly each year but within a predictable range. Getting the security deposit back in full took exactly 14 days, itemized, professional.

---

Meadow View Residences — 3 stars
Location is ideal — quiet street, safe neighborhood, 12 minutes to campus. The building itself is fine. The thing nobody tells you is that the parking lot turns into a swamp after heavy rain. I've had to wade through ankle-deep water to get to my car twice. Not a lease-breaker but bring rain boots or park on the street on rainy days. Management knows about it and keeps saying they'll fix the drainage. Two years in, it hasn't happened.

---

The Colonnade — 2 stars
Smelled musty when I moved in. I asked the landlord about it and he said "old buildings sometimes smell like that." Turns out there was mold inside the walls near the bathroom — didn't find this out until maintenance did work in month four and opened the drywall. Management handled the remediation once it was visible but I was breathing that for months. Always ask about mold history, even if the unit smells fine. Especially if it smells musty.

---

Sunridge Apartments — 4 stars
Good value for the area. Utilities are all-in except electricity, which averages about $60/month for a 1-bedroom. In-unit laundry, a functional gym (though basic equipment), and a package locker system that actually works. The building is well-lit at night which matters for walking home after late classes. Minor complaint: the walls are thin and you can hear neighbors, but that's true of most apartments at this price point.

---

Elm Grove Commons — 3 stars
The lease process here is pretty rigid — they require 3x monthly rent in income, which most students can't show, so you'll almost certainly need a co-signer. They were upfront about it during the application, which I appreciated. Once you're in, the management is fine and responsive. Just be prepared for the documentation requirements.

---

Northgate Student Housing — 1 star
The "all utilities included" in the listing was a lie. Water and trash were included. Electricity had a cap of $75/month included, and anything over that was billed separately — and my bills were regularly $140 in summer (AC) and $160 in winter. So I was paying $65-85/month extra on top of what I thought was an all-inclusive price. Read the lease very carefully for utility caps. If there's a cap, it will almost certainly be exceeded.

---

Riverside Place — 5 stars
Best apartment experience I've had. Responsive management, well-maintained building, fair deposit process. They gave us a room-by-room move-in inspection checklist on day one, we completed it together with the property manager, both of us signed it, and they honored it exactly at move-out. I got my full $1,200 deposit back in 12 days. Every landlord should operate like this.
