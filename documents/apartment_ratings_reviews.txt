Source: ApartmentRatings.com — Student-written reviews of apartment complexes near universities

---

Riverside Commons — 3/5 stars
Management is hit or miss. When we had a plumbing issue in month two, it took 11 days and three follow-up calls to get a plumber out. The unit itself is fine — decent size, in-unit washer/dryer which is rare at this price point. Parking is $85/month extra which they don't tell you upfront. Budget for that.

---

The Crossings — 2/5 stars
Noise is the big problem here. The building is right next to a main road and the windows are single-pane, so traffic noise starts at 6am. I bought earplugs. Also, the gym equipment on the third floor meant constant ceiling vibration in my second-floor unit. If you're a light sleeper or work from home, look elsewhere.

---

Maple Street Apartments — 4/5 stars
I've lived here two years and only have minor complaints. Maintenance responds within 48 hours, usually same-day for urgent issues. The heat is included in rent which makes winter totally predictable cost-wise. Downside: no dishwasher in the units, the parking lot floods in heavy rain, and the package room is tiny so packages get stacked and sometimes lost.

---

University Commons — 1/5 stars
Do not rent here. Management took my full $1,500 security deposit and sent an itemized list of charges that included things like "repainting entire unit" (it was in perfect condition), "professional carpet cleaning" (I left it cleaner than it was when I moved in), and "administrative fee for processing move-out" which is not a legitimate deductible expense. I disputed it in small claims court and won. Save yourself the hassle and rent somewhere else.

---

The Summit on Fifth — 5/5 stars
Best place I've lived in four years of college. Staff actually answers the phone. When my heat went out in January, someone was there in two hours. Rent includes water, trash, and gas heating, so my only variable utility is electricity (about $40/month). Not the cheapest option near campus but the lack of maintenance drama is worth every dollar.

---

Greenview Student Housing — 3/5 stars
Location is great — 8 minutes to campus by bike on a safe route. The units are showing their age (my bathroom tile has seen better days) but they work. Internet is not included and the only available provider in the building is one of the more expensive ISPs at $79/month. Factor that in. The management team is professional and they send detailed lease renewal notices 60 days out which I appreciated.

---

Lakeside Manor — 2/5 stars
Had a bedbug issue in month three. To their credit, management did treat it quickly once I reported it. But the experience of having bedbugs is genuinely miserable. When I was apartment hunting I didn't think to ask about pest history — I will always ask now. Before renting anywhere, ask the landlord directly: has this unit or building had a pest issue in the last 12 months?

---

Park Place Student Residences — 4/5 stars
Solid for the price. In-unit washer/dryer, the building has a bike storage room, and the management office is actually open on Saturday mornings which is when most students are around to deal with issues. Security deposit return was fast — got the full amount back within 10 days of move-out with no deductions. Move-in inspection sheet they gave us on day one was detailed and they honored it exactly at move-out.

---

Orchard Hill Flats — 3/5 stars
Electricity is not included and this building has terrible insulation. My electric bill was $180 in January because the heat ran constantly. If you're comparing this to a place where heat is included, add $100-150/month to the true cost comparison for winter months. Management is responsive but the building itself is old and drafty. Great location though.

---

Campus View Towers — 2/5 stars
The "utilities included" in the listing only means water and trash. Electricity, gas, and internet are all separate. My total utilities bill ran about $220/month, which made the rent much higher in practice than it appeared in the listing. Read listings carefully: "some utilities included" is not the same as "all utilities included."
